Our Story

Two kitchens, one table.

East Meets West began as a Sunday tradition between two grandmothers — one folding jiaozi in a Shanghai apartment, one rolling out dough in a Midwestern farmhouse. Their grandkids grew up trading recipes across time zones, and eventually decided the rest of the neighborhood deserved a taste.

We don't take shortcuts. No frozen wrappers, no commissary fillings. Every morning starts at 6 a.m. with a stand mixer, a rolling pin, and a long playlist.

— Bao, Mei, Jun & Lily Lin
